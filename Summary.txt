### 1. The Transformation: Chunks over Files
You learned that a Vector DB doesn't store "books"—it stores **Fragments**. Because LLMs have a limited "short-term memory" (Context Window), you practiced the art of **Chunking**.

*   **Concepts Learned:** `chunk_size` and `chunk_overlap`.
*   **Why it matters:** If you give the AI too much text at once, it loses focus. By breaking the book into 1,000-character pieces, you ensure the AI only "reads" the most relevant paragraphs.



---

### 2. The Translation: Embeddings
This is the "brain" of the operation. You used NVIDIA’s models to turn words into **Vectors**—long lists of numbers that represent meaning.

*   **Concepts Learned:** High-dimensional space.
*   **The Logic:** In a Vector DB, the word "King" and "Queen" are mathematically closer to each other than the word "Apple."
*   **Technical Detail:** When you query the DB, your question is converted into a vector, and the database finds the closest matches using calculations like **Cosine Similarity**:
$$\text{similarity} = \cos(\theta) = \frac{\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}}{\|\mathbf{A}\| \|\mathbf{B}\|}$$



---

### 3. The Library: Indexing and Persistence
You worked with **ChromaDB**, which acts as the "Long-Term Memory" for your AI.

*   **Indexing:** Instead of scanning every word (like a `Ctrl+F` search), the DB creates a map (an Index) of where every "meaning" lives.
*   **Persistence:** You learned that you can "save" this math to a folder (`persist_directory="db"`). This means you don't have to pay to re-embed your data every time you restart VS Code.



---

### 4. The Retrieval: RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
You successfully connected the "Memory" (Vector DB) to the "Brain" (NVIDIA Llama 3.1). This is the **RAG Pipeline**.

| Step | Component | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
